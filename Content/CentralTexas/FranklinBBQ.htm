Franklin Barbecue

General Information

Location: Austin

Date Established: 2009

Pitmaster: Aaron Franklin

Website: franklinbbq.com

Overview

Ah, yes. Franklin Barbecue. The "Holy Grail" of Texas barbecue joints. My wife and I went to Franklin on a whim during a trip to the Austin area in the summer of 2013. We knew there would be a long, long, loooooong line, so we arrived at 8:45 AM, a full two-plus hours before it opened. There were already about 40 people in line.

That is dedication! As you can see, they all brought chairs and books to pass the time. My wife and I thought ahead and brought our own folding beach chairs so we could sit during the multi-hour wait. They helped a ton!

About an hour before opening, someone from Franklin walked down the line, asking what and how much each person/party planned to order. The Franklin rep then estimated a point where the meat would run out and handed the person at that spot in the line a cardboard sign. Anyone in line behind the sign is out of luck for that day. C'est la vie!

Ron Recommends

I had the moist brisket and pork spareribs. I recall both being rather tasty, especially the brisket. Franklin has some interesting choices in sauces as well:

  • The usual tomato-based sauce that's prevalent throughout Texas.

  • A vinegar-based sauce that's more common in the Carolinas.

  • An espresso-based sauce that has a distinct coffee flavor that pairs well with smoked meat.

All three sauces were very good and are available at most major Texas grocery stores or online.

Ron Rates It

No debate on my end that the food at Franklin was good, and I enjoyed it very much. I'll give it four stars, but I don't get the INSANE hype around this place. I've done the three-plus-hour wait (four, actually, because there was another hour in line after we got inside the place). I don't need to jump through those hoops again, although we did have a celebrity sighting while waiting in line: Jared Padalecki, one of the stars of the TV show "Supernatural", was there with his family. I left him alone, of course. Dude just wanted a quiet meal with his family.

So, in closing, let me put it this way about Franklin Barbecue: good food but... "Been there, done that, got the t-shirt".

Quite literally:

I cannot stress this enough... the lines that form at Franklin are nuts and they form early. Get there several hours before it opens and bring a folding beach chair or rent one from the guy across the street. He charges $5 a pop, although he might have raised his prices since I visited.
