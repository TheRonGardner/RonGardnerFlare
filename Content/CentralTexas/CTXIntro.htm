Introduction

Hey there! Hope you're ready for my little tour of my favorite Central Texas barbecue joints!

I'm a born and raised Texan who spent his formative years in Central Texas, which spans - depending upon whom you ask - the following points of the compass:

  • Hillsboro to the north
  • Bryan/College Station (aka "Aggieland"; where I went to college) to the east
  • New Braunfels (where I went to high school) to the south
  • San Saba to the west

These points are pulled from the Wikipedia definition of Central Texas, although sentimental reasons made me alter its definition of the southern border to include my hometown of New Braunfels, which is the next town due south from San Marcos.

As part of this Flare certification project, I created a more expansive HTML version of this guide. That version includes the barbecue joints I've visited across the entire state of Texas. This PDF document focuses on the region that's closest to my heart, a region that is replete with amazing barbecue and even more amazing scenery.

Whenever I'm in Central Texas, I try to visit as many barbecue joints as I can, from Belton to Austin to Driftwood and points beyond. Before COVID, I visited the area at least every other year. Post COVID, it will be my mission to get back down there and continue my quest!
