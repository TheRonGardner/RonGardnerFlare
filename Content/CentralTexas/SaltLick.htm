The Salt Lick BBQ

General Information

Locations: Driftwood/Round Rock

Date Established: 1967

Pitmaster: Scott Roberts

Website: saltlickbbq.com

Overview

Besides Franklin Barbecue, The Salt Lick is probably one of the most famous barbecue joints in Texas. It's in an out-of-the-way location southwest of Austin, but it's totally worth the drive.

If you arrive later in the day, there can be pretty long lines to get in the door, so you might want to come early. However, if you do have to wait, there's usually something going on outside to pass the time, such as local musicians playing for the assembled crowd. Or you can just strike up a conversation with a nearby stranger and chat about other barbecue joints you've visited, which is a subject any true "'cue aficionado" would approach with abandon.

The Salt Lick serves food on three-sectioned plastic green plates.

My wife has a saying about them:

"Those little green plates make me happy!"

I wholly agree. A trip to the Salt Lick always makes me happy!

Ron's Review

The Salt Lick is well-known for its "Family Style" option, which is an all-you-can-eat meats-travaganza including the following:

  • Brisket (you can ask for either lean or moist or burnt ends)

  • Pork ribs

  • Pork sausage

  • Potato salad

  • Pinto beans

  • Cole Slaw

  • "Fixings" such as bread, pickles, jalapenos, and onions

A bit of advice... ask for a second round after you get through the first, even if you're stuffed to the gills and can't eat another bite. You can take the second round to go; the wait staff will happily give you to-go containers. They're pretty cool about that at The Salt Lick.

If you order the Family Style meal and aren't worried about the extra strain on your belt, also order the beef rib. This thing is freaking HUGE! It looks like something that would have flipped over the Flintstone's car. I mean, look at that thing!!!

One final suggestion... if - somehow - you have room for dessert after all that, go for the blackberry cobbler ala mode. I don't have much of a sweet tooth, but it is one of my favorite desserts. Ever. I don't know what The Salt Lick does with the base of the cobbler, but it seems to be some kind of sweet, cake-like cornbread supporting a thick, delicious blackberry compote. Add a scoop of vanilla Blue Bell ice cream, and it's pure bliss!

Let me put it this way... I don't have any pictures of the cobbler because I pounce on it every time it's set down in front of me.

Ron Rates It

My wife and I love The Salt Lick so much we had it cater our 2007 wedding in the nearby town of Wimberley. We REALLY love this place and make a point to go there every time we vacation in the area. Coming or going (and sometimes both), we ALWAYS fit in at least one trip to The Salt Lick!

This is a cash-only joint, so make sure you have some bills on hand before you set foot in there. If you don't, there is an on-site ATM. However, be prepared to pay some serious fees to get that cash.
