Overview

My wife and I head to northeastern Texas several times throughout the year. Sometimes we head down Interstate 20, sometimes down US Highway 80. A while back, we discovered CowBurners in the small town of Mineola. We stopped by on a lark and did not regret the decision at all. It's on our agenda to stop by whenever we are anywhere near Mineola.

Ron's Review (12/29/22)

During my most recent visit, my wife had the BBQ Frito Pie with a side of Hatch green chile mac and cheese. I tried some of the Frito pie, and it was amazing! I'd rank it close to one of my favorite barbecue Frito pies, the one from Stanley's Famous Pit BBQ in Tyler.

I had the two-meat plate with the following:

  • Moist brisket

  • Pepper Jack pork sausage

  • Pinto beans

  • Hatch green chile mac and cheese

Meats

The moist brisket was excellent

Pepper jack sausage

Sides

The pinto beans were... decent. I recall them being better during previous visits. Full disclosure, I grew up eating my mom's phenomenal pinto beans, so I am admittedly hyper-critical when it comes to my pintos. The beans I tried this time weren't seasoned much and were almost al dente. Perhaps that was by design to offset the beans offered in the Frito pie. That being said, I will try them again the next time I return and offer a second opinion.

The Hatch chile mac and cheese, however... yeah! It was most definitely the better of the two sides I tried. It was made with cavatappi pasta and not the usual elbow macaroni. The sauce was creamy and smooth, with the bacon and Hatch green chiles providing a great complement.

Dessert

Ron Rates It

When we travel down 80 and feel like stopping in Mineola, a trip to CowBurners is always on the agenda.

Mineola is still a pretty small town, so CowBurners isn't terribly large on the inside. If you show up later in the day, it might be dodgy to get a table. You might want to arrive early. On the flip side, CowBurners is also a taproom, so you could nab a beer at the bar if you have to wait for a table.
