Central Texas BBQ Joints I've Visited

Here's a list of every barbecue joint I've visited in the Central Texas area. My wife and I vacation regularly in the area, so we've visited quite a few places.

You can't throw a rock in any direction in this state without hitting at least one barbecue joint. I have not come even close to scratching the surface of places to visit.

That being said, I've compiled this list of every barbecue joint I've visited throughout my Texas travels. I'm sure I've forgotten one or two along the way, but these are the ones that stood out in my memory... for good, bad, or somewhere in between. If I've written a review for a joint, I've included a link to it.

Symbols and Terms in This List

The following table explains the symbols of terms I've used to designate certain items in this list:

Symbol/Term

Explanation

1 to 5 stars (* to *****)

My rating for a place, as explained in the My Rating System section.

If the name of a joint has a link, I've written a detailed review for the place. The link opens my review for that joint.

Superscript Top 50 (Top 50)

Texas Monthly Magazine has included this place on the current iteration of its highly-respected "The Top 50 Barbecue Joints in Texas", which is updated every 4 years. If a place is in the top ten, I included its ranking on the list.

For example, Farmer's Branch: Cattleack Barbeque Top 50: #6 means that Cattleack made the #6 spot on the current Texas Monthly list.

Texas Monthly provides a numeric ranking only for the joints it placed in the top 10.

Superscript Top 50 Sat (Top 50 Sat)

The place is on the Texas Monthly list, but I've been to a different (satellite) location and not the location mentioned on the current Top 50 list.

For example, Houston: Pinkerton's Texas Pit BarbecueTop 50 Sat means that while I've been to the original location in Houston (which, when I went in 2018, was on the list), I have not been to the satellite location in San Antonio that replaced the Houston location on the current (as of 2021) list.

Superscript HM (HM)

The place is not on the current Texas Monthly Top 50 list, but it is on Texas Monthly's current "Honorable Mentions" list, which spotlights another 50 BBQ joints that also need recognition.

Forward slash (/)

I've been to multiple locations of that barbecue joint, with the original location listed first.

For example, Driftwood/Round Rock: The Salt Lick BBQ means I've been to both the original Driftwood and the spin-off Round Rock locations of The Salt Lick.

National chain

One or more locations are available in 5 or more US states (not just Texas).

Texas chain

One or more locations are available in multiple cities in Texas outside the Dallas/Fort Worth area.

DFW area chain

This barbecue joint has multiple locations in the greater Dallas/Fort Worth area.

So, if you include the 1 satellite location I've visited, I've been to 9 joints out of 50 on the current Texas Monthly list, which was released in late 2021. If you include the Honorable Mentions list, I've been to 17 joints out of 100. Not too shabby!

List of Central Texas BBQ Joints by Region

I've separated the joints by region, similar to how I set up the top navigation menu. Some folks might disagree with me on my designations (e.g., what exactly constitutes "Central Texas"), but this is how I see it.

Actually, the funniest breakdown of the different regions of Texas comes by way of Richard Linklater's hilarious dark comedy "Bernie", starring Jack Black, Shirley MacLaine, and Matthew McConaughey:

"The Five States of Texas"

This scene cracks me up every time I watch it, if only because he actually lists seven different regions of Texas. It hits pretty close to home for me, though. I'm closely related to several guys that are exactly like that character.

Fun fact... that scene was filmed at Zimmerhansel's BBQ in Smithville. I've been there, of course!
