How to Play Tic-Tac-Toe for Two Players

Purpose

These instructions explain how to play the pen-and-paper version of tic-tac-toe using two human players.

Prerequisites

Before you can begin playing, you need the following:

  • Two human players
  • A pen or a pencil
  • A sheet of paper
  • A level playing surface on which you can draw

Objective

The first player to draw a continuous line of 3 Xs or Os in the grid (connected vertically, horizontally, or vertically) wins.

How to Play

  1. Using the pen or pencil, draw a 3 x 3 grid (3 rows, 3 columns) on the piece of paper. The grid should resemble a hashtag (#).
  2. Determine who goes first. Player One will play Xs, and Player Two will plays Os.
  3. Player One is not required to play Xs, but these instructions consider this a best practice.
  4. Player One, draw an X in any of the empty squares in the grid.
  5. Player Two, draw an O in any of the remaining empty squares in the grid.
  6. Repeat steps 3 and 4 until one of the following happens:
    • Either of the players draws connects three of his or her symbols (vertically, horizontally, diagonally)
    • All nine grid squares are filled and neither player has 3 in a line

Tips

The tips in this section can help you become a better Tic Tac Toe player.

  • If you can, put your symbol in the center square. This gives you the best chance for connecting 3 of your symbols in a line.
  • If the other player puts a symbol in the center square, put your symbol in one of the corner squares. This gives you the best chance of connecting 3 of your symbols without going through the center square.
  • If you can't connect 3 of your symbols in a line, try to prevent your opponent from doing so. If you can't win, there is no shame in playing defense.
