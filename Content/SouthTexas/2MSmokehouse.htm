2M Smokehouse

General Information

Location: San Antonio

Date Established: 2016

Pitmaster: Esaul Ramos

Website: 2msmokehouse.com

Overview

"BBQ worth waiting for".

"Worth every minute".

"Best BBQ in Texas".

These are just a few of the accolades heaped on this "Tex-Mex barbecue" joint located on the southeast side of San Antonio. All this is definitely well-deserved praise. However, I think the phrase that best describes this place is prominently displayed on the front of the building:

Barbecue con ganas

My Spanish is a tad rusty, but I believe it roughly translates to "barbecue with passion". That's not an exaggeration on their part. You can tell the people at 2M really love what they do and create some fantastic barbecue with a slightly different twist.

My wife and I visited here on a whim during a trip to San Antonio on a late summer Saturday afternoon in 2019. Some of the items we wanted to try were sold out (definitely a good sign we're somewhere worthwhile), but that didn't bother us at all. It's part of the gamble when you arrive at a well-known Texas barbecue joint later in the day.

Ron Recommends

I went with the moist brisket, which was smoky, tender, and fatty in the best, most delicious way. I wanted to try the sausage made with pork, poblano chiles, and Oxaca cheese, which sounded amazing, but they were sold out that day. Undaunted, I opted for the beef sausage. I'm not usually a fan of all-beef sausage, I prefer the texture of all-pork or a mixture of pork and beef, but 2M's had a great flavor that allayed my concerns.

The sides did not disappoint. I ordered the pinto beans, chicharoni macaroni and cheese, and elotes. They were all delicious, but the elotes really stood out. It had a smoky, creamy, and sweet taste that didn't overwhelm the rest of the meal. Elotes is a side you don't see in a lot of barbecue joints outside of South Texas, so it was nice to try something different.

Ordinarily, I'd provide a picture here, but I was so hungry when I got to 2M I forgot to take a pic until halfway through the meal. The picture ended up looking like it came from a crime scene, so I will spare you.

Ron Rates It

My wife and I eat quite a bit of barbecue when we're in the Hill Country/San Antonio area on vacation, but we both agree that we're definitely heading back to 2M Smokehouse during our next trip to the Alamo City!

If you can get there on the first Sunday of a month, 2M offers barbacoa. I have never had the chance to try it there, but I hear 2M's is legendary.
